A change is happening There

There is a paradigm shift in the software industry, and enterprises both large and small recognize that in order to scale, optimize, achieve high ROI and increase profitability, they must adopt a digital transformation strategy.

Digital transformation is the use of emerging technology to fundamentally improve productivity and performance while reducing costs. Enterprises going through a digital transformation will represents a 2.2 trillion dollar market in 2018.

By 2020, 50% of the Global 2000 will see the majority of their business depend on their ability to create digitally-enhanced products, services, and experiences.

By 2020, all enterprises' performance will be measured by a demanding new set of digital transformation-driven benchmarks. At least 1/3 of leaders in every industry will fail to clear these digital transformation hurdles.

Problem

Many enterprises claim to have completed their digital transformation or are in the process of achieving it.

But have they achieved their goal? To be successful, it is necessary to achieve 4 important Key Performance Indicators (KPIs):

ROI (Increase Profitability) Speed Speed Speed (Agility) Quality (Customer Satisfaction) Standardization (Globalization)

As Clayton Christensen from Harvard Business School mentioned, the reason it is so difficult for existing firms to capitalize on disruptive innovations is that their processes and their business model, [which] make them good at the existing business, actually make them bad at competing for the disruption.
